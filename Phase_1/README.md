# Phase 1: Conceptualization & Initial Simulation (Months 1 & 2)

This phase covers the foundational research, theoretical kinematics, and preliminary digital design for AmphiGlide's biomimetic locomotion system. The primary goal was to map the kinematics of natural undulating fin propulsion and translate it into a mechanical linkage system.

## 📂 Directory Structure

* **`/CAD_Files`**: Contains early 3D models, assembly drafts (SolidWorks/Siemens NX), and `.step` files for the core drive mechanism and internal chassis footprint.
* **`/Code`**: Initial pinout planning, baseline communication scripts for the Raspberry Pi 4 to ESP32 interface, and preliminary servo testing logic mapped out by M. Ali Shahbaz Khan.
* **`/Simulations`**: Preliminary boundary conditions and meshing setups for Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) in ANSYS Fluent to evaluate potential fin profiles.
* **`/Images`**: Reference photos of the initial electronic components, early whiteboard sketches, and kinematic equations.

## 🎯 Phase Milestones Achieved

1. **Mechanical Linkage Design:** Drafted the preliminary mechanical linkages required to translate standard rotary servo actuation into the undulating, sinusoidal movement needed for the fin.
2. **Hardware Architecture Scope:** Finalized the selection of core processing units (Raspberry Pi 4 and ESP32) and mapped out the theoretical load for the custom power distribution system.
3. **Biomimetic Research:** Established baseline parameters for testing fluid-structure interaction to inform our eventual material selection for the propulsion fin.
