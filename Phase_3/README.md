# Phase 3: Assembly, Integration, & Material Innovation (Months 5 & 6)

This phase documents the physical integration of all sub-systems into a single, cohesive prototype. A major milestone during this period was the innovative sourcing of our fin material to maintain biomimetic properties while strictly adhering to budget constraints.

## 📂 Directory Structure

* **`/Mechanics`**: Documentation detailing the assembly of the 3D printed chassis and the mounting process of the linkage arms.
* **`/Electronics`**: Final wiring diagrams and code pushes merging the Raspberry Pi 4 high-level control with the ESP32 low-level motor actuation.
* **`/Images`**: Documentation of the physical wiring process, integrating the 2 batteries and 4 power buses, and photos of the complete internal hardware assembly.

## 🎯 Phase Milestones Achieved

1. **Material Innovation:** Sourced and integrated a repurposed gym mat to act as the undulating fin. This provided flexibility and material properties remarkably close to expensive silicone rubber, allowing for highly effective propulsion at a fraction of the cost.
2. **Hardware Integration:** Successfully mounted the complex servo array and fully wired the dense internal electronics, including routing the 4 dedicated power buses through the chassis.
3. **Full System Handshake:** Achieved stable communication between the Pi 4, the ESP32, and the physical servo array, resulting in full-chassis sinusoidal actuation.
